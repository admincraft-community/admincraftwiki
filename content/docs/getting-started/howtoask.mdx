<Callout type="info" title="The Golden Rule">
The quality of answers you receive often directly reflects the effort you put into asking your question. Well-structured questions show respect for others' time and expertise.
</Callout>

## Before You Ask

<Steps>
<Step>
Search existing resources thoroughly:
- Community forums and FAQs
- Official documentation
- Previously asked questions
- Tutorial videos and guides
- Related discussion threads
</Step>

<Step>
Gather essential information:
- Specific details about your situation
- Error messages or issues (if any)
- Your current setup
- Steps to reproduce the problem
- Things you've already tried
</Step>

<Step>
Prepare your evidence:
- Take screenshots if needed
- Collect relevant logs
- Document your setup
- Create examples of the issue
</Step>
</Steps>

## Question Structure Template

```markdown
## What I Want to Achieve
[Clear explanation of your goal]

## Current Setup
[Describe your relevant environment/situation]

## The Problem
[What isn't working or what you need help with]

## What I've Tried
[List your previous attempts]

## Specific Questions
[Your actual questions]

## Additional Information
[Any other relevant details]
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

<Cards>
<Card title="❌ The Help Vampire">
Bad:
"Hello"
"Anyone here?"
"Can someone help?"
"Is this the right place to ask?"

Good:
"Need help setting up permissions for WorldGuard regions on my Minecraft server. Already checked documentation and tried basic flags. Details below."
</Card>

<Card title="❌ The Fragment Bomber">
Bad:
Message 1: "Hi"
Message 2: "I have a problem"
Message 3: "With my server"
Message 4: "It keeps crashing"

Good:
"Server crashes on startup after installing EssentialsX. Latest crash log and server details provided below. Already checked for plugin conflicts."
</Card>

<Card title="❌ The Solution Assumers">
Bad:
"How do I edit the config.yml?"

Good:
"Looking to change spawn protection radius. Found config.yml but unsure which settings to modify. Current server settings listed below."
</Card>
</Cards>

## Following Up

### Best Practices

<Cards>
<Card title="During Discussion">
- Respond promptly
- Test suggestions quickly
- Provide clear feedback
- Stay engaged
</Card>

<Card title="After Resolution">
- Share the solution
- Mark as resolved
- Thank helpers
- Document for others
</Card>
</Cards>

<Callout type="info" title="Resolution Sharing">
Always share what solved your problem, even if you figure it out yourself. Future searchers will thank you!
</Callout>

## Community Etiquette

<Cards>
<Card title="Be Patient">
- Give helpers time to respond
- Complex issues need research
- Different timezones exist
- Avoid repeat posting
</Card>

<Card title="Show Effort">
- Research before asking
- Format questions clearly
- Provide requested info
- Stay engaged in discussion
</Card>

<Card title="Be Respectful">
- Thank people for help
- Accept different approaches
- Follow community rules
- Help others when possible
</Card>
</Cards>

<Callout type="warn" title="Remember">
